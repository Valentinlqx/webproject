# Ambience Sound Library — Download Shopping List

A curated list of sound effects to download (AudioHero, Freesound, Sonniss GDC packs, BBC Sound Effects archive, ZapSplat…) to build a virtually unlimited number of ambience/vibe loops in Ambience Forge.

## How this list works

Every ambience is built from three ingredient types. Download across all three and the combinations multiply:

| Type | What it is | Length to get | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **BED** | Continuous texture, plays the whole loop | 30–120 s | rain wash, wind, room tone, crowd murmur |
| **EVENT** | Recurring one-shot, triggered every few seconds | 1–10 s | bird call, thunder clap, creak, church bell |
| **ACCENT** | Rare, distinctive one-shot for identity | 1–15 s | wolf howl, foghorn, train pass-by, owl |

**The math:** 2 beds + 2 event sets + 1 accent per mix. With ~40 beds, ~50 events and ~30 accents you get tens of thousands of distinct, plausible combinations — before touching volume, pan, intensity, or the procedural + AI layers.

**Quality checklist when downloading:**
- WAV preferred (44.1/48 kHz, 16/24-bit); high-bitrate MP3 acceptable for beds
- **Dry recordings** (minimal baked-in reverb) — the app's shared reverb glues layers together
- No music, no melodic content (kills loopability and mixability)
- Beds: consistent level throughout, no fade-in/out, no one-off events baked in (a bed with a dog bark in it will telegraph the loop point)
- Events/accents: clean attack, natural decay, silence at both ends
- Prefer mono for events (pan them yourself), stereo for wide beds

## Recipe cheat-sheet (combine as: 2 beds + 2 events + 1 accent)

- **Cozy cabin storm**: fireplace bed + rain-on-window bed + log pops + wall clock + distant thunder
- **Haunted manor**: old-house room tone + haunted wind + house creaks + floorboards above + 3 slow knocks
- **Elven forest dawn**: deep forest bed + brook bed + songbirds + woodpecker + distant elk
- **Thieves' harbor night**: harbor-hull water + quiet-suburb night + boat creaks + footsteps pass + buoy bell
- **Derelict spaceship**: engine-room hum + vent hiss + hull groans + servo movement + distant klaxon
- **Desert ruins**: desert wind + cold metallic drone + rock debris + banner flaps + distant dragon
- **Rainy noir city**: rain-on-pavement + distant city hum + wet car pass-bys + pigeon flutter + far siren
- **Deep dungeon**: cave tone + sub swell drone + echo drips + chain rattles + metal door slam

## Mixing tips (matches how Ambience Forge works)

1. **Loudest thing defines the scene** — pick ONE dominant bed, keep the second bed ~40% quieter.
2. **Events sell the loop as "alive"** — 2 event types at irregular intervals beat 5 constant ones.
3. **One accent max** — its rarity is what gives the scene identity; more than one gets noisy.
4. **Pan events, center beds** — matches the app's per-layer pan slider.
5. **Low-pass = distance** — the AI layer's Brightness slider does this; darker = further away.
6. **Under 3 identifiable sources at once** — the ear stops believing crowded mixes.
7. Keep every downloaded file **dry**; let the app's shared reverb place everything in the same space.
